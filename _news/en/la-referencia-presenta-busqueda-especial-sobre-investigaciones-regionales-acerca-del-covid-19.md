Recently the Confederation of Open Access Repositories (COAR) **presented [its recommendations for resources on COVID-19 in repositories](https://www.lareferencia.info/en/component/k2/item/264-recomendaciones-de-coar-para-recursos-sobre-covid-19-en-repositorios).** COAR emphasized the need for repositories and regional networks to work collaboratively, thus seeking to make research on the subject more accessible and visible, with the objective that **governments, public and private institutions** can make use of them in order to build new knowledge that adds to the fight against COVID-19.**

Following these guidelines, **LA Reference now shares on its main page [a parameterized search](https://bit.ly/36X6rfj)** that concentrates COVID-19 research in Latin American repositories.

This is a **dynamic collection** that **grows** every week. It currently consists of around **500 academic documents** that account for the production deposited in network repositories in **Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Ecuador, El Salvador, Mexico, Peru and Uruguay.**

In this way, LA Reference joins the global efforts of Open Science **to build and democratize knowledge related to this disease**, hoping to collaborate with the institutions and people who, in their daily lives, work **to overcome the health, social and economic crisis that COVID-19 has generated.**
