<p><a href="https://www.unesco.org/en">UNESCO</a>, <a href="https://www.lareferencia.info/en/">LA Referencia</a> and <a href="https://libereurope.eu/">LIBER</a> are hosting a joint webinar on Tuesday 3rd October (16:00 CEST: 8 a.m. Costa Rica/México, <strong>9 a.m. Panamá/Perú/Colombia/Ecuador, 10 a.m. Chile, 11 a.m. Argentina/Brasil/Uruguay</strong>) aimed at providing a state-of-the art analysis of the Open Science monitoring methods and tools currently developed in Europe. The event will introduce inclusive monitoring of Open Science, in line with the 2021 <a href="https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000379949">UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science</a>.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">About the event</h2>



<p>The webinar will take inspiration from the similarities between Latin American and European approaches to Open Access (see this study <a href="https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/8a4852c6-bca3-11ed-8912-01aa75ed71a1">in English</a> and <a href="https://op.europa.eu/es/publication-detail/-/publication/8a4852c6-bca3-11ed-8912-01aa75ed71a1/language-es/format-PDF/source-281856386">in Spanish</a>) Attendees will gain insights for developing Open Science monitoring methods, criteria, sources and tools.</p>



<p>The main benefits for attendees will be:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Awareness of Open Science monitoring implementation momentum.</li>



<li>Knowledge of the UNESCO vision of Open Science monitoring and the tools needed to follow the implementation and fulfilment of the <a href="https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000379949">UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science</a>.</li>



<li>An introduction to the state-of-the-art  monitoring methods and tools currently developed in Europe for measuring Open Science progress and actions (OpenAIRE monitoring vision, challenges, tools and methods; France and Finland’s experiences with monitoring national and institutional frameworks and tools.)</li>



<li>An international alignment of reproducible methods, measurement criteria, tools and practices for assessing and monitoring Open Science progress.</li>



<li>Opportunities to develop partnerships, collaborations and networking between regions and/or countries.</li>



<li>Opportunities to develop their personal, institutional, and national network(s).</li>



<li></li>
</ul>
